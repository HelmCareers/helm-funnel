Fair questions

You're skeptical. Fair. Here are the straight answers.

Straight up: Helm is new. You'd be one of the first 25 people to use it — that's exactly why launch pricing exists and why the first 25 members get a direct line to the founder. Early is the deal.

Can my current employer find out I'm looking?
Not through Helm. During setup you blocklist your current employer — and anyone else you name (parent companies, sister divisions, that one recruiter who talks) — and Helm will never surface, research, or prepare an application to them. Nothing is posted publicly, no "open to work" flags are touched, and no application goes anywhere without your individual approval.
Is this one of those spam bots that gets accounts banned?
No — it's designed as the opposite. The ban-risk tools submit applications autonomously, in bulk, under your identity. Helm never submits anything. It prepares everything and you approve each application individually — designed so a human (you) performs every send, the opposite of the bulk-bot pattern platforms ban. There is no bulk-blast mode. That's a design decision, not a setting.
What makes this different from LazyApply, LoopCV, and the other cheap tools?
Volume versus quality. Those tools fire one generic résumé at hundreds of jobs — that's why they're $20/month — and why recruiters have learned to spot and skip the pattern. Helm tailors every single application to the specific posting, from your real experience, and sends only what you approve. Ten excellent applications beat five hundred spam ones — especially at the $100k+ level, where your name and reputation are on every submission.
What if the AI makes something up on my application?
This is the core engineering rule of the product: Helm never guesses. If a posting asks for something that isn't in your résumé or answers — a certification date, a clearance, a skill — it comes back to you as a question, never a fabricated answer. Applications with open questions are locked and cannot be approved or batch-sent until you answer.
How does the Landing Guarantee work?
Simple: run your Sprint or Pro plan properly — keep your goal active and review your briefings — and if you don't have a signed offer by day 90, your next 90 days are free. No forms to fill, no fine-print gymnastics; if you were in the game and it didn't land, we stay in it with you at no charge. (It doesn't stack with abandoning the search — the briefings you approve are the mechanism.)
What if I try it and it's just not for me?
Then you pay nothing. Every plan comes with a 7-day no-questions money-back guarantee — one email, full refund, no retention call, no exit survey. A week of briefings is enough to know if Helm is for you, and we'd rather refund fast and keep your respect than argue over $399.
What if I land a job in week two?
Congratulations — that's the outcome you paid for, arriving months early. A faster landing is worth far more than any unused plan time: every month saved is a month of the new salary in your pocket. Monthly cancels in one click; Sprint and Pro are single payments — no recurring billing to escape.
Who sees my résumé and data?
You, and the pipeline that tailors your applications. Your data is never sold, never used to train models for other companies, never shared with employers without an approval from you. Export or delete everything, anytime, from your account page.
Why is launch pricing limited to 25 people?
Because the launch period is how the engine gets tuned before wide release: 25 members is a group small enough that every piece of feedback actually changes the product. The first 25 keep their launch rate for as long as they keep their plan, get a direct line to the founder, and get real input into the roadmap in exchange for that feedback.
